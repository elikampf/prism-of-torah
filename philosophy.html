It Started with Sushi and Scotch

Seven years ago, I was visiting family in Toronto, catching up with close childhood friends over sushi and smooth scotch. I shared a Torah thought that felt meaningful to me, and honestly, I was just hoping it would resonate with them too.

After I finished, one friend said something that changed everything: "You should do this every week. Send us audio clips we can listen to while commuting."

That casual suggestion‚Äîborn from friendship and good food‚Äîbecame the seed for everything you see here. Sometimes the most meaningful journeys start in the most ordinary moments.

Who I Am

I'm Asaf Aharon Prisman, and I live in Ramat Beit Shemesh with my family. I learn at Kollel Beis Hillel, teach a daily Daf Yomi shiur, and spend my days thinking about how to make Torah wisdom accessible to people living real, busy lives.

My path took me from Toronto to yeshivos in Israel‚ÄîBirkat Moshe in Maaleh Adumim and Netivot Olam in Bnei Brak‚Äîwhere I developed my approach to learning. But the real education happened when I realized that all that depth means nothing if it doesn't connect to how people actually live.

What Makes This Different

I meet you where you are.

Not everyone starts at the same place in their Jewish journey. Not everyone has the same background, time, or learning style. But everyone can grow. My job is to meet you exactly where you are and provide the next achievable step toward where you want to be.

Everything has three parts.

Whether it's a podcast episode or a written piece, you'll always get:

  • Deep teaching from classical sources, explained clearly
  • Practical takeaway you can actually use in daily life
  • Memorable story that makes the concept stick

Most Torah resources give you one of these. I give you all three, every time.

Contemporary language, timeless wisdom.

I explain ancient concepts through modern understanding. No jargon, no assumptions about your background, no spiritual ideas that stay abstract. If I can't explain it in language that makes sense to someone commuting to work, I haven't understood it well enough myself.

What This Is Really About

The Hebrew title of my work is "Shomer HaPardes"‚Äîfrom the verse in Nechemiah where Asaf was the keeper of the king's orchard. Someone who cultivated and protected precious growing things.

The English title, "Prism of Torah," plays on my surname but captures something deeper. Just as a prism reveals the full spectrum of light hidden in a single ray, Torah learning should reveal infinite depth in every verse, every concept, every life situation.

Torah isn't meant to be compartmentalized into "religious time." It's meant to be the lens through which everything else makes sense‚Äîyour relationships, your challenges, your daily decisions, your biggest dreams.
